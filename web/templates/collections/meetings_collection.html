Who are ministers meeting?

Since 2010 all government ministers have published a quarterly record of their meetings with outside organisations. Information is given on who met with whom, the date of the meeting and a brief description of the purpose of the meeting.

In reality, the ministerial meeting logs provide only a tiny window on discussions held with corporations and other outside interests. Not all meetings are recorded; and of those that are, few details are given on what has been discussed, with meetings frequently logged merely as ‘catch-up’ meetings, or ‘general discussions’. Records are also often published months late, not at all, or in remote corners of departmental websites.

Ministerial meeting data can sometimes provide a starting point for further investigation.
