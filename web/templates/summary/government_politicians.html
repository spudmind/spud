PROFILE

John Hutton

The ex-Barrow and Furness MP, John Hutton, joined the upper house in July 2010, after a successful run as a senior Cabinet member in the previous Labour government.

His career since has seen him take on many commercial jobs. Perhaps most notable is his role as head of the lobbying group, the Nuclear Industry Association, which afforded him the privilege of co-writing the foreword to the Coalition government’s nuclear strategy.

A health minister under Tony Blair from 1998 to 2005, Hutton oversaw radical changes to the NHS. In 2014, he landed a directorship with Circle Holdings, the investment arm of private healthcare provider Circle, famous for controversially being the first private company to take over the running of a whole NHS hospital.

Hutton is also a paid adviser to tax avoidance experts, PWC, which is also heavily involved in public sector reform: it claims to have ‘acted on more privatisations than any other financial adviser’. The former defence secretary (2008-09) is also a paid consultant to US arms giant Lockheed Martin (also interested in NHS outsourcing work); and Bechtel Corporation, the US’s largest construction and engineering firm.

Hutton has also been involved with the global lobbying firm APCO, although this was never declared in his official register of interests. Until recently, he was listed as a member of APCO’s board of senior advisers, which helps clients with, among other things, political intelligence, government relations, reputation management and policymaking processes. It singles out its expert counsel on government systems for defence and public service reform. Hutton said he ‘doesn’t concentrate on’ the areas, but declined to expand on this, or why he had not declared the interest.
